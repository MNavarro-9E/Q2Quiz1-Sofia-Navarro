Philippine Historical Figures

Image Info Historical Significance
The first President of the Philippines, also known as Emilio Aguinaldo, led revolutionary forces during the Philippine Revolution and declared Philippine independence from Spain on June 12, 1898. He became the head of the revolutionary government but later faced the challenge of fighting against American colonization during the Philippine-American War.
  • ✦ Played a crucial role in the revolution during the war against Spain
  • ✦ Declared independence for the Philippines
  • ✦ National Hero
Philippine patriot, founder and leader of the Katipunan society, Andres Bonifacio was also known as the "Father of the Philippine Revolution". He also founded the Kataas-taasan, Kagalang-galangang Katipunan ng mga Bayan, or widely known as the KKK.
  • ✦ Was a multifaceted intellectual and a political activist
  • ✦ Exposed the abuses and injustices committed by the Spanish colonial government and the clergy
  • ✦ National Hero
In the Filipino expatriate community, Juan Luna de San Pedro y Novicio Ancheta was one of the leading members. This community was mainly held in Europe along with luminaries such as Jose Rizal (the Philippine National Hero). These expatriates desired to reform Spain’s rule of the Philippines, and laid the intellectual foundation for rebellion against Spain.
  • ✦ He was one of the first recognized Filipino artists
  • ✦ Won a gold medal at the National Exposition of Fine Arts in Madrid in 1884 for his magnum opus, the Spoliarium
  • ✦ National Hero
Maria Josefa Gabriela or Gabriela Silang met a man named Diego Silang, that possesses great intelligence, and was greatly determined to fight for the Philippines. They got married in 1757. In 1762, she decided to join the revolt against the Spaniards with Diego. Unfortunately, he was assassinated. Gabriela wanted to avenge him by continuing to fight even if he wasn't by her side anymore. She was then the new leader of this revolt and continued to lead her men throughout the revolt.
  • ✦ First female leader of the revolutionary movement against the Spaniards
  • ✦ National Hero
José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda or simply, Jose Rizal was a novelist, patriot, poet, physician, and essayist. He is greatly acknowledged for his first novel, The Social Cancer (Touch Me Not/Noli Me Tangere) and its' sequel, The Reign of Greed (El Filibusterismo)
  • ✦ Was a multifaceted intellectual and a political activist
  • ✦ Exposed the abuses and injustices committed by the Spanish colonial government and the clergy
  • ✦ National Hero
